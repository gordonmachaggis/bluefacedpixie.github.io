Small cycling rant

I almost posted this morning after getting excited on the way to work.

I would like to start this by saying that spring is here in Seattle and that riding to work is the most invigorating and relaxing commute anyone could wish for, except when it’s a crazy videogame with your blood as quarters. Recently it’s been the second flavour.

It’s been a strange last two days commuting – first of all the Elliott Bay Park is closed, so for the last two mornings I’ve had to detour via 15th Ave W – hellishly fast, full of honking drivers that bring out the most defensive (read: aggressive) cycling techniques known to man. Then I had a double puncture and failed repair last night and had to end up on the bus home (!). And then this morning some crazy truck tried to wipe me out on the pedestrian crossing. But it was still gorgeous tonight and I rode 4th Ave (my old “heading to Queen Anne” route through the busy centre of downtown Seattle) north and then did Dexter/Fremont/Ballard using the Burke-Gilman. Everyone was once more thankfully sane.

I’m hoping for a quick restoration of the Elliot Bay trail, and whatever craziness has infested the local drivers (I blame daylight savings time ending) to abate quickly.

Rant over.
