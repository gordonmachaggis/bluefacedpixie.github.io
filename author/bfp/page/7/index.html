22nd October 2002 – Siena, Italy

….Bed Bugs! The shock, the horror. We survived. Funnily enough there was a National Strike that day, which meant some things were closed. On day 104 we went to the Uffizi which was one of the more awesome galleries/museums of the trip, to say nothing of just walking around in one of the most photogenic towns of our trip yet (Florence!).

After a day trip to see a leaning tower we headed to Siena, starting to play chicken with the late-Autumn European weather but I am totally blanking on it, so will need to go find some old pictures.

17th October 2002 – Florence, Italy

This is an installment – 3 countries, recovered wallets, wild crazy vomiting, the 100 day mark, 2 train overnights and Italy, Italy, Italy.

We stayed in a lovely hotel in Barcelona (Hotel Jardine) and took the train from Spain to France during which the afore-mentioned vomiting occurred (and yet we still ended our stay in Nice with eating some of the most iffy food of the whole trip (cheap bouillabaisse)). But then we moved on to Venice.

Venice was great, although we had some food challenges and looking back I find it somewhat hard to believe we were only there one night but then we went to Florence (I’m assuming by train). It’s nice in Nice – 15th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Internet Cybercafe, Nice, France]

We are just at the end of three relaxing days in Nice. We arrived early Sunday morning on an overnight train from Barcelona. Gordon was quite ill when we arrived and spent the first day mostly in bed. (He’s feeling better now, thanks to Danika’s attentive care :)). Nice has been lovely and sunny- it’s even been warm enough to lay on the beach in bathing suits! We’ve seen a few more art museums and walked the length of the shore endlessly. Unfortunately, we can’t afford to stay here; Nice is far more expensive than Spain. The other downside of this cute seaside town is the extraordinary amount of dog poo on the ground! We’ve adjusted to walking with our eyes on the ground at all times to avoid stepping in it – Gordon has been unlucky…

Since our last news, we spent three days in Barcelona, which Danika deemed her favorite Spanish city (G is partial to Toledo). Despite some difficulties with lodging, we had good food, more cheap wine and saw lots of cool Gaudi architecture. The city seems to have quite a buzz to it and we enjoyed walking up and down the main street – Las Ramblas. It even had a Starbucks as well as a good Picasso museum. En-route to Barcelona we spent a day in Granada, though it was slightly disastrous – got soaking wet at the Alhambra, spent hours trying to find an Internet cafe, lost Danika’s wallet (later recovered), and so on.

In any case, we’re off to another new country tonight. Yet another overnight train – this one to Venice, Italy (perhaps the only place that’s even more expensive than Nice!). We’ll be spending roughly two weeks in Italy with our typically open schedule – the main objectives include Florence, Tuscany and Rome.

Danika sends her thanks to everyone for their wishes this past week. It’s nice to hear from you directly even though we don’t always have time to write back to you individually.

Oh yes, and for all those in suspense, Gordon did get a haircut. Not in Sevilla, but in Barcelona. He still doesn’t know the Spanish for it, but ‘mucho’ is for when you want more off, and ‘grande blanco’ has something to do with the increasing amount of grey hair he has.

Au revoir,

D and G

A busy Spanish week saw us moving from Madrid through Toledo and Sevilla to Granada. On the way Danika’s grandfather passed away, which tested our travelling resolution and we went to Flamenco for his distant funeral (we last saw him at the family reunion in Buffalo – well worth that East Coast dash). Holy Toledo Batman!!! – 7th October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Dodgy Internet Cafe, Sevilla, Spain]

We’re now in our third Spanish city. We’ve been to Madrid, Toledo and currently we’re in Sevilla. Our plan is to stay here for a couple of nights and then head out. Probably to Granada and then Barcelona.

Since our arrival in Spain we’ve started to passively smoke about 30 cigarettes a day because all Spaniards smoke like industrial chimneys. This is much worse than Ireland where the Irish only smoke like little chimneys.

In Madrid we went to the Prado and the Sofia museums to see most of the major Spanish artwork and also hung around a fair bit. We had tapas almost every night, and started to get into the Spanish way of staying up, and getting up, late.

We took the train to Toledo (the ancient capital of Spain) and it was great. It was full of twisty little streets and Moorish architecture and castles and lots of very bloody Christian history. We also got out best food so far in Spain – although the tapas have been very good we’ve been struggling to get good big meals we like.

While we were in Toledo we also got some bad news from home as Danika’s grandfather passed away. He had been ill for some time, but it was still a shock when it actually happened. After trying to see how feasible it was for Danika to go home for a bit we decided to continue travelling, and came to Sevilla on Sunday. Danika has called home a few times and our thoughts are with the whole family.

Today it’s Monday, so everything touristy is closed. This is giving us some time to do chores, laundry etc. Depending on how brave he’s feeling Gordon may even attempt to get a haircut.

Adios Amigos,

Gordon & Danika

Dear Mom, Guinness is good for you! – 1st October 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Martin’s House, Dublin, Ireland]

The Emerald Isle, Land of Guinness and Leprachauns, The Four-Leafed Clover, etc…..

Well our time in Ireland is nearly over, and we decided to send this newsletter before we head off to Madrid this afternoon. This way we manage to send a letter from each country we’ve been too!

Since our last email (from the library Gordon used to live in as a kid) we’ve completed our grand tour of Scotland. For the last few days we visited some of Gordon’s friends and relatives, and in doing that also managed to go to the west coast of Scotland in Ayrshire and to the home of golf in St. Andrews. Fully stocked on fish suppers, good Indian and haggis neeps and tatties (to say nothing of oatcakes, Irn Bru, venison, dodgy Scottish beer, cider, caramel shortbread, Cadbury chocolate, Kit Kats, etc) we flew from Edinburgh to Dublin.

We’ve had a grand time in Ireland. Staying with Martin and Jane we’ve been to the Guinness Brewery, seen Trinity college, the Irish National Museum and Gallery. We also took a long day trip across Ireland to Galway and flew (6 minutes each way) to the Aran Islands on the west coast. These islands were impressive in their rocky desolate nature – although it’s no wonder these are the people who invented Aran sweaters. Apart from donkey and boat, we used almost all forms of travel for this day trip: car, train, coach, plane and public bus. Impressive, eh?

For our time in Scotland and Ireland we’ve had nice dry weather. For people not from these parts, and who did not suffer the dismal endless rain of ‘summer’, let me assure you that this was very welcome and we feel that the weather gods have smiled on us for this part of the trip. We’re definitely looking forward to donning summer gear again, as the daily temps in Madrid are around 29 C (84 F) this time of year.

Now we head off to Spain for our next experience: strange people who don’t understand English/Scottish/American no matter how slowly and loudly you talk.

We’d like to leave you with one of our favourite Irish quotes (from the Irish Literary Pub Tour, and the distinguished Brendan Behan (sic)):

“The Irish Man is the only Man in the world who would crawl over twenty naked women to reach a pint.”

Adios amigos,

Gordon & Danika

I would walk 500 miles… – 23rd September 2002

(Being a re-post of a ten-year old Yahoo groups newsletter update)

[Kilsyth Public Library, Scotland]

..or at least drive 1000 more.

We have been blessed with excellent weather for our time in Scotland. We’ve managed to see many of the big attractions and put another 1,000 miles of driving in – this time in Gordon’s mum’s wee car. Our first couple days were spent in Kilsyth at Gordon’s childhood home. We were treated to lots of yummy soup and caramel shortcake – Margaret’s specialties. Spent a couple of days in Glasgow – the highlights being a fabulous meal at G’s favourite restuarant and a night of traditional Scottish dancing (ceilidh dancing). Unfortunately, Gordon’s kilt is in Seattle, but it was an exciting evening anyway.

For the past week we’ve been on a grand tour of Scotland. We started with Ben Lomond and then spent the night near Fort William. We continued on to the Isle of Skye, which is infamous for bad weather. It didn’t rain, which means it was quite good, and we had fun hiking in the Quirang. We also had ‘a wee dram’ at the Talisker whisky distillery. Oh yeah, the scenery was quite good too. After two nights in Skye, we hit the quaint town of Ullapool, where we saw the excellent Scottish Ensemble perform. Then a breathtaking drive through Northen Scotland and a stay in one of the northernmost towns (Durness) before heading south to Inverness. Two nights in Edinburgh, including a most terrifying ghost tour, and we’re back in Kilsyth!

Danika’s having a great time here and frequently proclaims her newly adopted country – Scotland! We’ve been practising accents and she’s mastered such local tongue twisters as “It’s a braw bricht moonlicht nicht, the nicht, it’s a richt ya ken?” and “There’s a moose loose aboot the hoose”. Two more days in Scotland and we’re off to Dublin to see Martin and Jane, and the Guinness brewery.

Until the next time,

Slainte Mhath,

Danika and Gordon
