Ride: The Last Tiger of ’06 Season

Just over a week ago (before I headed out on some work travel and after the Mac headed to the Genii), Morgan, Forest and I hit Tiger on the last weekend of the season.

Tiger traditionally is an April 15th – October 15th trail, although oft-times inclement or beneficial weather will change those parameters. This year there is a good chance that the season will be extended, but it’s open to the wiles of rangers and cannot be trusted.

I try to make a tradition to ride as close to each end of the season as possible and this year managed the second last day. We rode Iverson first, confirming it’s position as the hardest and most ‘perfect’ trail. It was a nice gray day so I had packed my Nikon and the fixed 50mm lens. I snapped a few shots and will hopefully post them on shutterfly or flickr soon. Morgan was carrying some injuries and after the fireroad climb begged off the downhill trails. That was understandable and Forest and I made short work of Preston and NWTT to join Morgan in the car park. After which some kobe burgers and microbrews at the Rogue Brewery in Issaquah set me in good stead for my overnight flight to Finland.

Bye Tiger, see you next year (although I hope I manage to bike a little more this year!).

Ride: Tiger Tiger Tiger

Did a quick blast around le Tiger today.

Unlike a few weeks ago (when I pulled off an unblogged ride in hot, hot weather), the conditions were good today – overcast, around 60° and dry.

I rode with the Furiously Fast Forest who blew me away on Preston although we were pretty companionable on NWTT, and for the first time in a while I had a couple of tumbles on Tiger. One was a sloppy line and the other was a washout due to going a little fast, but in both cases no damage was done.

Cycling!

Despite variable-to-crap weather I set a good track record cycling to my new job. Although it’s shorter than the trek to Amazon (9 miles instead of 16 roundtrip) it’s far more tempting to bike to AOL: the bus routes don’t work well, parking is expensive and the route itself is mostly bike lane – including a bit of the Burke-Gilman Trail and scooting across the Fremont Bridge. With sunny weather this week I hope I can keep it up before winter closes in.

Ride: Friday Tiger

Had a personal day on Friday, which translated as playing hooky at Tiger.

It was perfect dry sunny weather. For variety I rode the low Iverson/”Fat Hand” trail which is a good bit more challenging than the normal two trails I ride. My little heart and lungs had a good workout, and I actually had to use some bike skills.

I then headed up the fireroad for a pretty standard Preston/Northwest Timber Trail combo, getting done in a couple of hours and heading home for leftover Indian food.
