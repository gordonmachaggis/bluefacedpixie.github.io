Burgers at Kings

I know Danika is about to blog about this, but I thought I’d mention I’m becoming a bit of a burger gourmet. Which makes no sense, as I wasn’t brought up on McDonalds – for crying out loud Wimpy was a yearly treat!

Anyway we tried burgers at the new Ballard place yesterday: King’s Hardware.

Burgers: 3/5, bland meat, soft buns, pleasant composition and toppings
Fries: 4/5, in a hand-shaved-I’m-glad-you-exist kinda way
Beer: 3/5, in terms of selection solid, focus on PBR – poor.

Nice Burger

I don’t see why Danika should get to review all the food, especially as she wasn’t there ðŸ™‚

Post-Tiger last night we went to the Issaquah Brewpub – which I never knew existed in old town Issaquah. The Brewpub has a few of it’s own beers but has also been bought by the Rogue chain, which ensures a larger supply of micro-brews and a glossy standard of service and environment (I don’t know what it was like before).

I had a rather stellar Kobe Beefburger with blue cheese and (shock!) real fries! (no more Hilltop tavern with no deep fat fryer etc!).

Soft, tender, very full of flavour – a winner!

The beers were pretty good too. Adrian swears by the chocolate stout, and I tried a Chipotle Ale (nice but a little lighter than I was looking for) and an IPA-like beer I missed the name of (I think it’s the brewed-on-location beer) but which was very refreshing. Food: Maximilien’s in the Market

I normally leave it to Danika to do the food posts but tonight’s food warrants a mention.

A classic over-hyped French restaurant in the tourist-friendly Pike Place Market. That’s all I ever thought Maximilien’s was, even though I’d had lunch and snacks there. I even went with Mum while doing the Market last September.

We went tonight to have some food, managed to sneak into the upper “view” bar after sunset but before the happy hour (5-7) finished and had frites, 2 doses of mussels, French onion soup, bread and a couple of beers and wines. All of which were great. And the bill was less than $30. This is not an over-hyped tourist restaurant – even though it is a tourist-trap.

I think we have to go back.

Maximilien’s in the Market: 4/5

Aaah, Besalu

We’re considering naming our first child Ulaseb.

It’s hard to imagine that the last time we were are at Cafe Besalu was Christmas Eve. But with their long holiday and then our trip to Whistler it was nice to be back to the buttery routine this weekend.

We walked over in the sunshine after many days of rain, had Ham and Swiss Pastry and a couple of Pain au Chocolat and coffee. Aaah, Besalu. The Pain au Chocolat rocked – 3 pounds of butter each I think.
